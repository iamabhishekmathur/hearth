# Hearth Product Demo Video v1

Status: Draft ready for capture  
Primary cut: 75-90 seconds  
Audience: AI power users, team leads, CTOs, IT, and security-minded founders

## Strategic Angle

The video should not feel like a feature tour. It should feel like a before/after:

> One teammate's useful AI work becomes a shared operating system for the team.

The attention hook is the tension every AI-forward team already feels:

- A few people are getting real leverage from AI.
- The work is trapped in individual chat histories and scattered tools.
- The rest of the team starts from zero.
- IT either tolerates shadow AI or locks it down.

Hearth is the third path: capture the work, make it reusable, keep the memory, and govern the system.

## Primary Narrative

### 0:00-0:08 - Hook

On-screen:

> Your best AI work is trapped in one teammate's chat.

Voiceover:

> Your team is already using AI for real work. But the useful parts are trapped in chat tabs, docs, Slack threads, meetings, and vendor histories.

Product surface: Chat, "Enterprise Beta Launch Review".

### 0:08-0:22 - Breakthrough

On-screen:

> One power user pulls the launch together.

Voiceover:

> In Hearth, that work starts as a shared AI session. The AI pulls context from launch notes, customer feedback, meeting notes, GitHub, and Slack, then turns it into blockers, decisions, follow-up, and a recommendation.

Product surface: Chat session with launch review messages.

### 0:22-0:34 - Work Becomes Visible

On-screen:

> The follow-up becomes work, not vibes.

Voiceover:

> The tasks do not disappear into the bottom of a chat. Hearth turns the next steps into a visible board the team can own.

Product surface: Workspace kanban.

### 0:34-0:48 - Pattern Becomes Reusable

On-screen:

> The workflow becomes the team's starting point.

Voiceover:

> The launch review gets saved as a reusable skill. Now the next person does not start with a blank prompt. They start from the workflow that already worked.

Product surface: Skills, Enterprise Launch Review.

### 0:48-0:60 - Recurring Work Runs Itself

On-screen:

> The recurring loop becomes a routine.

Voiceover:

> And if the work repeats, Hearth turns it into a routine: weekly launch-risk digests, procurement checks, customer-feedback triage.

Product surface: Routines, Monday Launch Risk Digest.

### 0:60-0:72 - Team Adoption

On-screen:

> The rest of the team can discover and reuse it.

Voiceover:

> The activity feed shows what is working across the organization, so useful workflows spread without another meeting.

Product surface: Activity feed.

### 0:72-0:84 - Memory And Decisions

On-screen:

> The company keeps the context.

Voiceover:

> The memory and decisions belong to the organization, not a model provider's chat history.

Product surface: Memory and Decision Graph.

### 0:84-0:96 - Governance

On-screen:

> Govern AI without locking it down.

Voiceover:

> Admins get the controls they need: compliance packs, scrubbing, auditability, and self-hosted infrastructure.

Product surface: Compliance.

### Final Frame

On-screen:

> Make your AI power users' breakthroughs your team's starting point.

Subline:

> Hearth is an open-source AI workspace for teams.

CTA:

> Self-host it. Bring your own models. Own the memory layer.

## Production Notes

- Use captions as the primary narrative. Voiceover can be added later.
- Keep cursor motion slow and deliberate.
- Avoid showing every tab. Every screen must earn its place in the story.
- The product should look like a working operating layer, not a dashboard gallery.
- The compliance screen should end the story because it resolves the buyer objection.
