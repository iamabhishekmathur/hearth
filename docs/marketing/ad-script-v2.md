## HEARTH AD SCRIPT v2 — "Nobody Shares"

**Format:** Talking to camera like you're telling a friend a story over coffee. No setup, no qualifying question. Phone-shot aesthetic. Jump cuts mid-sentence (natural, not polished).

---

### 1. THE CALLING (0:00-0:05)
**[Mid-thought energy. Like you just sat down and started talking.]**

> "So your company bought everyone AI tools, right?"

**Text overlay:** `your company bought AI tools`

---

### 2. THE WOUND (0:05-0:20)
**[Slight smirk. You've seen this movie before.]**

> "Yeah. Same thing happened at every company I've talked to. Like 10 people actually use it. Everyone else opened it once, asked it something dumb, got a weird answer, and never came back.

> But here's the thing that actually kills me — the people who ARE getting crazy value from it? They just... keep it to themselves. Not on purpose. There's just no way to share what's working."

**Text overlay:** `the wins stay invisible`

---

### 3. THE SECRET (0:20-0:32)
**[Drop the energy. Almost conspiratorial, like sharing a realization.]**

> "And I kept thinking — it's not that AI isn't good enough. It's obviously good enough. The problem is everyone's starting from scratch every single time. Like imagine if every person at your company had to re-discover Google from zero. That's what's happening with AI right now."

**Text overlay:** `everyone starts from zero`

---

### 4. THE BRIDGE (0:32-0:48)
**[Energy picks back up. Show, don't pitch.]**

> "So we built this thing — it's called Hearth, it's open-source — and the whole idea is stupid simple."

**[Quick screen recording — one flow, not three]**

> "Someone on your team figures out a workflow that works — like pulling weekly sales numbers and writing a summary — they save it as a skill, and now anyone in the company can use it in one click. And it actually knows your company's stuff — your Slack, your docs, your meetings."

**[Screen shows: skill shared -> activity feed -> teammate installs it]**

> "So instead of 10 people being good at AI, suddenly everyone is. Because they're building on each other."

**Text overlay:** `one person figures it out. everyone gets it.`

---

### 5. THE INVITATION (0:48-0:55)
**[Casual. Almost throwaway, like it's obvious.]**

> "It's free, it's on GitHub, your data stays on your servers. Link's in my bio if you want to check it out."

**Text overlay:** `Hearth -- open source -- link in bio`

---

## What changed from v1

| v1 problem | v2 fix |
|---|---|
| Opens with a qualifying filter ("if you're an engineering leader...") | Opens with a shared experience everyone relates to |
| Uses product language: "harness," "multiplayer-first," "proactive work intake" | Uses human language: "keep it to themselves," "starting from scratch" |
| Three rapid-fire screen demos feel like a product tour | One continuous flow that tells a story (Sarah builds -> team gets it) |
| "The models are good enough. The harness isn't." — tagline, not conversation | Google analogy — same idea, but it lands in casual speech |
| Closes with feature list (self-hosted, git clone, 5 minutes) | Closes like you're recommending a restaurant to a friend |
| 60 seconds of pitch density | 55 seconds that feel like 30 because it's a story |
