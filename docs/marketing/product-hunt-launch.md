Hey Product Hunt


I'm Abhishek, the maker of <b>Hearth</b>.


I built Hearth for leaders who are stuck between two bad AI choices.


On one side, there is shadow AI: employees paste customer data, roadmap notes, deal context, source snippets, and internal decisions into private AI tabs because it helps them move faster.


On the other side, there is AI lockdown: companies block the tools, lose the productivity gains, and still do not get a better system for how AI should be used at work.


<b>Hearth is the third path: a governed AI workspace for shared chat and agent work.</b>


Teams get a place to use AI across Slack, meetings, docs, and tools. C-level, VP, CTO, CISO, and IT leaders get the controls they need before AI spreads into real work:


<li>company policies attached to AI work</li>
<li>sensitive-data controls before context reaches an LLM</li>
<li>human approval gates before agents send, update, or publish</li>
<li>audit-friendly activity for agent work and decisions</li>
<li>organization-owned memory instead of scattered vendor histories</li>
<li>Hearth Cloud for speed, or open-source self-hosting for infrastructure control</li>


A few things I'm especially excited about:


<b>1. Governance before rollout</b><br>
Hearth gives leaders a control layer before teams adopt AI across customer, product, engineering, support, and operations workflows.


<b>2. Policies attached to real work</b><br>
Admins can block, redact, warn, or route sensitive context before it reaches an LLM. Agent work can wait for approval before it touches customers, teammates, or systems of record.


<b>3. Company-owned memory</b><br>
Context, decisions, workflows, outputs, approvals, and audit history should belong to the organization, not disappear into individual AI accounts.


<b>4. Cloud or self-hosted</b><br>
Use Hearth Cloud for managed speed, or self-host the open-source core when infrastructure, storage, memory, and model control matter. Bring your own model path where appropriate.
