## HEARTH AD SCRIPT v1 — "The Dusty Seats"

**Format:** Founder/narrator talking to camera, casual setting (desk, laptop open). Quick cuts. Bold text overlays on key phrases. Screen recordings spliced in for The Bridge.

---

### 1. THE CALLING (0:00-0:08)
**[Direct eye contact. Conversational, slightly confrontational tone.]**

> "If you're an engineering leader or head of ops at a company with 50 to 500 people... and you've already bought ChatGPT Enterprise or Copilot seats for your team..."

**Text overlay:** `50-500 person team? AI seats purchased?`

---

### 2. THE WOUND (0:08-0:22)
**[Lean in. Knowing expression — "I've seen this before."]**

> "...then I already know what happened. You're paying for 200 seats. Maybe 15 people actually use it. The rest tried it once, got a mediocre answer, and went back to Googling.

> And here's the part that really hurts — the 15 people who ARE getting value? Their breakthroughs die with them. Nobody shares what works. Every person starts from zero, every single time."

**Text overlay:** `200 seats. 15 active users. $0 shared knowledge.`

---

### 3. THE SECRET (0:22-0:36)
**[Shift energy. Lean back. Confident, almost relieved.]**

> "The problem was never the AI model. GPT-4, Claude, Gemini — they're all brilliant. The problem is the *harness* — the integrations, the context, the shared knowledge around it.

> You don't need a smarter model. You need a platform where one person's breakthrough becomes everyone's baseline. Automatically."

**Text overlay:** `The models are good enough. The harness isn't.`

---

### 4. THE BRIDGE (0:36-0:50)
**[Cut to screen recording — fast, punchy, 3 quick demos]**

> "This is Hearth. It's open-source. Watch what happens:"

**Screen demo 1 (3s):** *Sarah in Sales builds a skill that pulls pipeline data from Salesforce and writes a weekly forecast. One click — she shares it to the whole org.*

**Text overlay:** `Sarah builds it once ->`

**Screen demo 2 (3s):** *The Activity Feed lights up. Three teammates install the skill. A notification: "Trending: Weekly Pipeline Forecast."*

**Text overlay:** `-> 47 people have it by Friday`

**Screen demo 3 (3s):** *Slack channel shows a Hearth routine posting a morning briefing. A teammate clicks "Powered by Hearth — set up yours."*

**Text overlay:** `It spreads itself.`

> "One person's 10-minute workflow becomes the whole company's operating system. The floor rises for everyone."

---

### 5. THE INVITATION (0:50-0:60)
**[Back to camera. Warm, direct.]**

> "Hearth is free, open-source, and self-hosted — your data never leaves your servers. You can go from `git clone` to your first real result in under 5 minutes.

> Link's in the bio. Stop paying for dusty seats."

**Text overlay:** `github.com/hearth-ai/hearth`
**End card:** Hearth logo + "Open-source AI for teams" + GitHub star count

---

## Production Notes

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Tone** | Anti-corporate. Founder energy. Think Loom's early ads or Basecamp's "Reconsider" talk. Not polished — *credible*. |
| **Hook metric** | The "200 seats, 15 active users" line is the psychological anchor. Every buyer has felt this exact pain. It should hit in the first 8 seconds (after the qualifying question). |
| **B-roll options** | Dashboard screen recordings, Slack notifications appearing, the kanban board with tasks auto-populating, the skill marketplace. |
| **Music** | Lo-fi or ambient electronic. Subtle — don't compete with the voice. Slight energy lift at "This is Hearth." |
| **Captions** | Mandatory. 80%+ of Instagram video is watched on mute. Bold keyword highlights on the text overlays. |
| **CTA variant for paid ads** | Replace "Link in bio" with a direct link card / "Tap to star on GitHub" — optimize for the GitHub star as the micro-conversion. |
| **Length variants** | Cut The Bridge to one demo for a 30s version. Expand to 3 full demos for a 90s YouTube pre-roll. |
