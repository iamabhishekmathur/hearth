# Decision Graph

Applies to: Hearth Cloud and self-hosted Hearth.

The Decision Graph captures what the organization decided, why, who was involved, what depended on it, and what happened afterward.

[[toc]]

## What It Captures

Decisions can be created manually or detected from chat and meeting context. A decision can include:

- Title.
- Reasoning.
- Domain.
- Participants.
- Confidence.
- Sensitivity.
- Alternatives.
- Dependencies.
- Outcomes.
- Review status.

## Browsing Decisions

Use the Decisions page to inspect the timeline, search decisions, review graph relationships, and open details.

## Review Queue

Auto-detected decisions should be reviewed before they become trusted organizational context. Reviewers can confirm, edit, or dismiss suggestions.

## Outcomes

Outcomes record what happened after a decision. This helps Hearth learn which patterns work and gives future conversations better context.

## Patterns and Principles

Hearth can cluster related decisions into patterns and distill organization-level principles. These can be fed back into agent context so future work is informed by past choices.

## Chat Integration

Users can ask Hearth about past decisions, dependencies, or principles. When relevant, the AI can surface decision context in chat and tasks.

For capture and review controls, see [Decision Graph Administration](/admin/decision-graph).
